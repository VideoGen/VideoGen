Abstract

Text-to-video generation have recently shown promising progress. While this is still a challenging task due to uncontrollable content, temporal consistency, computational complexity and video clarity, etc. In this paper, we propose DreamVideo, a text-to-video generation method conditioned on text and generated image using progressively cascaded diffusion models in the latent space. In particular, we use a image generated by StableDiffusion as a additional condition so that the generated video content is more controllable. Besides, the cascaded generation decomposes the entire task to different levels of models, so that different models focus on different issues, the minimum resolution model for the motion and the next two for the super-resolution, making the generated video more consistent and more stable. In addition, we propose a flow-guided video latent interpolation diffusion model, compared with pixel-level interpolation, which can produce arbitrary multiple of interpolation frames but the results won't get blurred. Finally, an enhanced video-level decoder is used to make the generated video clearer and more stable. Since models are trained in the latent space, we reduce the resources required for training while maintaining the clarity of the generated videos.
